# KM Framework Selection

---

### 1. KM Problem Identification
- The primary knowledge management problem this project addresses is the loss of organizational memory within the academic environment. Currently, the valuable insights, methodologies, and findings from graduating students' capstone and thesis projects are siloed in individual hard drives or physical archives. This knowledge is hard to find for future student batches, leading to a lack of continuity, redundant research efforts, and a missed opportunity for students to build upon the work of their predecessors.
### 2. KM Frameworks Considered
- To address this problem, two primary KM frameworks were evaluated:
  The SECI Model (Nonaka & Takeuchi): This model focuses on the continuous "spiral" of knowledge creation through four stages: Socialization (Tacit to Tacit), Externalization (Tacit to Explicit), Combination (Explicit to Explicit), and Internalization (Explicit to Tacit).
  Wiig’s KM Model (Karl Wiig): This model emphasizes that for knowledge to be valuable, it must be organized specifically according to how users will apply it. It focuses on the KM Cycle (Building, Holding, Pooling, and Applying knowledge) and categorizes knowledge into forms (Public, Shared, Personal) and types (Factual, Conceptual, Expectational, Methodological).
### 3. Chosen Framework and Rationale
- The team will utilize Wiig’s KM Model as the intellectual foundation for the platform.
  While SECI is excellent for explaining how knowledge is created through social interaction, Wiig’s model is more suitable for an archival and portfolio platform because it prioritizes the application and organization of existing knowledge assets. Since our project is essentially an "Organizational Memory" repository, Wiig’s emphasis on "Holding Knowledge" in repositories and "Pooling Knowledge" for collaborative access aligns perfectly with the goal of making past projects accessible for future use.
### 4. App Feature Mapping to Wiig’s KM Cycle
- The application’s core features map directly to the components of the Wiig KM Cycle:
  
| Framework Component | App Feature | KM Rationale |
| ------------------- | ----------- | ------------ |
| Building Knowledge | Project Upload & Metadata Form | Allows students to codify and model their project knowledge by submitting documents, abstracts, and tags. |
| Holding Knowledge | Archive Database & Repository | Provides a centralized location to cumulate and archive knowledge in computerized bases for long-term retrieval. |
| Pooling Knowledge | Search, Filters, & Categories | Facilitates accessing and retrieving knowledge by allowing users to navigate the repository via taxonomies (e.g., by department or year). |
| Applying Knowledge | Project View & Reference Portal | Supports students in using established knowledge to solve their own research problems and identify "special knowledge" sources for their capstones. |

---

This framework ensures that the "Student Project Showcase & Archive" is not merely a file storage site, but a strategic system designed to enhance the internalization and application of academic knowledge across student generations.
